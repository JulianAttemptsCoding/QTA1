# SOURCES.md — Annotated bibliography

Compiled 2026-07-02 from primary sources (arXiv, SSRN, journal pages, vendor docs).
Grouping: (A) LLM-agent market simulation, (B) validity threats for LLM backtests,
(C) classical ABM/simulators, (D) prior art for Idea 2, (E) data sources.
Each entry: what it is → why it matters to AgoraSim.

## A. LLM-agent market simulation (Idea 1's home literature)

1. **TwinMarket: A Scalable Behavioral and Social Simulation for Financial Markets** —
   arXiv:2502.01506 (ICLR 2025 workshop version on OpenReview).
   LLM investor agents with social interaction; reproduces stylized facts and
   self-fulfilling dynamics. → Closest realism-track precedent; AgoraSim's RQ1
   battery should be comparable to their reported facts. Delta: they simulate a
   market; we test *fidelity to a measured retail crowd* (Robintrack) and
   *incremental information* on real small caps with contamination controls.

2. **StockAgent: LLM-based stock trading in simulated real-world environments** —
   arXiv:2407.18957. LLM agents trade under external factors (rates, news);
   studies emergent behavior, not real-stock alpha. → Precedent for
   persona/heterogeneity design; no post-cutoff prediction claims — our RQ3 gap.

3. **ASFM — "Simulating Financial Market via Large Language Model based Agents"** —
   arXiv:2406.19966. Agent-based simulated financial market with LLM traders.
   → Same family; useful for prompt/mechanism comparisons.

4. **Lopez-Lira (2025), "Can Large Language Models Trade? Testing Financial
   Theories with LLM Agents in Market Simulations"** — arXiv:2504.10789 (also SSRN).
   Open framework: heterogeneous LLM agents (value, momentum, market-maker
   archetypes) in a discrete-round continuous-double-auction/call market.
   → The closest open-source engine; our call-auction round structure follows
   this convention (D-01). Delta: we point the crowd at *real* retail-heavy small
   caps and score against realized returns and Robintrack behavior.

5. **Agent-Based Simulation of a Financial Market with Large Language Models** —
   arXiv:2510.12189. LLM-agent market sim; notes price-impact behavior differences
   across model sizes (smaller models produce measurable impact patterns).
   → Supports using small models as behaviorally interesting agents (D-08).

6. **MASS: Multi-Agent Simulation Scaling for Portfolio Construction** —
   arXiv:2505.10278. Scales LLM-agent count for portfolio decisions; reports
   improvements with scale. → Motivates our N-agent scaling-curve experiment (A-402).

7. **Shachi: modular framework for LLM agent-based modeling of emergent collective
   behavior** — arXiv:2509.21862. → Methodology reference for controllable ABM-with-
   LLMs experiment design.

8. **Behavioral Consistency Validation for LLM Agents (trading-style switching)** —
   arXiv:2602.07023. Tests whether LLM agents hold assigned personas under market
   pressure; cites Henning et al. (2025) finding LLM traders act "too rational" and
   under-produce retail-like noise. → Direct warning for RQ2: persona drift and
   excess rationality are failure modes; our decision-entropy diagnostic (F-06) and
   persona-consistency checks come from here.

9. **When Agents Trade: Live Multi-Market Trading Benchmark for LLM Agents** —
   arXiv:2510.11695. Live benchmark; LLM agents' realized trading is mediocre.
   → Calibrates expectations for RQ3 (honesty box, PLAN §1).

10. **Integrating LLMs in Financial Investments and Market Analysis: A Survey** —
    arXiv:2507.01990. → Field map; positioning citations for the writeup.

## B. Validity threats (the two papers that can kill naive versions of this project)

11. **Lopez-Lira & Tang, "Can ChatGPT Forecast Stock Price Movements?"** —
    arXiv:2304.07619; Journal of Financial Economics (forthcoming/2025 version v6).
    GPT-based news sentiment predicts next-day returns (~tens of bps/day long-short
    pre-cost in headline specs); predictability is **concentrated in smaller stocks**
    and persists in post-training-cutoff samples. → Establishes (a) the single-LLM
    baseline we must beat (D-09a) and (b) that small caps are where text-based
    predictability lives — the reason our universe is retail-heavy small caps.

12. **Glasserman & Lin, "Assessing Look-Ahead Bias in Stock Return Predictions
    Generated by GPT Sentiment Analysis"** — arXiv:2309.17322 / SSRN.
    Anonymizing company identifiers in headlines materially changes in-training-
    window performance → memorization/look-ahead is real and measurable; also
    documents a "distraction effect" from company recognition. → Source of our
    anonymization A/B arms (D-06, C-3) and the named-vs-alias gap metric (A-304).

13. **Look-Ahead-Bench: standardized benchmark of look-ahead bias in point-in-time
    LLMs for finance** — arXiv:2601.13770. → Recent confirmation the problem is
    first-order; probe-style tests inform C-2.

## C. Classical simulators / infrastructure

14. **ABIDES (Byrd, Hybinette, Balch): agent-based interactive discrete event
    simulation** — arXiv:1904.12066; ABIDES-gym at ICAIF'21. NASDAQ-style
    high-fidelity multi-agent market simulator. → The non-LLM gold standard; we
    deliberately do NOT rebuild it (K-3) — our auction is a one-file call market.

15. **MarS: a Financial Market Simulation Engine Powered by Generative Foundation
    Model** (Microsoft) — arXiv:2409.07486. Order-level generative "Large Market
    Model". → Shows the *generative-model* route to realistic order flow; AgoraSim
    is the *interpretable-agent* route (rationales are readable JSON) — a
    positioning contrast for the writeup.

## D. Prior art for Idea 2 (function-dictionary / analog forecasting) — parked

16. **Lo, Mamaysky, Wang (2000), "Foundations of Technical Analysis"** — Journal of
    Finance 55(4); NBER WP 7613. Kernel-regression pattern detection; patterns carry
    *some* incremental information; **profitability is not established**. → The
    ceiling for shape-conditioned forecasting set 25 years ago.

17. **Park & Irwin (2007), "What do we know about the profitability of technical
    analysis?"** — Journal of Economic Surveys 21(4). Reported profits shrink under
    proper out-of-sample tests, costs, and data-snooping corrections. → The survey
    verdict Idea 2 must overturn.

18. **Case-based-reasoning / trend pattern-matching for stock prediction** — e.g.,
    "A New Trend Pattern-Matching Method of Interactive Case-Based Reasoning for
    Stock Price Predictions" (Sustainability, 2019-era) and successors. Hit-ratio
    evaluations, no cost-aware alpha. → Idea 2's exact template already exists.

19. **SAX line (symbolic aggregate approximation)** — Lin & Keogh et al.; example
    application "SAX-ARM" (deviant event patterns in multivariate series).
    → Symbolizing windows then matching = Idea 2 with a different alphabet.

20. **Pattern-graph / complex-network price-pattern prediction** — e.g., Cao et al.
    2019 (Complexity); "Pattern graph tracking-based stock price prediction using
    big data" (ScienceDirect). → More of the same family; marginal accuracy claims,
    no DSR-grade validation.

21. **Ultra-high-frequency predictability limits** — entropy-based randomness test
    (arXiv:2312.16637); Hurst-exponent EMH tests (arXiv:0712.1624, 0708.4178).
    → At minute scale, measurable structure ≠ tradeable edge after costs.

## E. Data and tooling

22. **Alpaca Market Data docs + community threads** (docs.alpaca.markets; forum:
    "IEX or SIP with a free account", "IEX feed historical data"; Postman API
    listing incl. News endpoint). Free/Basic plan = real-time IEX + 15-minute-
    delayed SIP; historical bars older than 15 minutes are queryable (feed
    behavior per key verified at G0). News API is Benzinga-sourced with multi-year
    history (depth verified at G0). → Primary price/text source; F-03/F-04 hedges.

23. **Robintrack** (robintrack.net — "Full Robintrack Database Download";
    GitHub Ameobea/robintrack). Robinhood popularity (users-holding) scrape,
    2018-05-02 → 2020-08-13, ~8.5k tickers, publicly downloadable. Academic use:
    Welch (2022, JF) "The Wisdom of the Robinhood Crowd"; Barber, Huang, Odean,
    Schwarz (2022, JF) attention-induced trading. → RQ2 ground truth + CALIB
    universe filter; era is calibration-only (D-05).

24. **Boehmer–Jones–Zhang–Zhang (2021, JF) retail-order imputation** — the standard
    retail-flow measure, requires TAQ (paid). → Cited to justify why we use free
    proxies instead; not used.
